Fried Rice

Fried rice is my favourite delicacy in the world. Fried rice is a dish of cooked rice that has been stir-fried in a wok or a frying pan and is usually mixed with other ingredients such as eggs, vegetables, seafood, or meat. It is often eaten by itself or as an accompaniment to another dish.

Ingredients Amount
Rice 3 cups
Meat 500g
Chicken 500g
Vegetables As desired
Liver 250g
Vegetable oil A cup
Salt, Seasonings and spices To taste

Procedure:

  1. Wash, season, boil and fry the meat
  2. Wash, marinate and fry the chicken
  3. Wash the rice and set aside
  4. In a cooking pot, add oil followed by onions, garlic and ginger then allow to fry till it releases its flavour
  5. Add the washed rice into the pot and allow to roast. Add the stock from the meat or chicken into the rice, add salt, seasonings and spices as desired.
    Allow to cook for about 30 minutes.
  6. While the rice is cooking, add oil into a saucepan followed by onions, garlic and ginger allow to fry till its crunchy not burnt
  7. Add pieces of the meat, chicken and liver into pan
  8. Add carrots, greenpeas, green pepper, sweetcorn and allow to fry
  9. Add salt and spices to taste.
  10. Into the cooked rice, add the vegetables, mix together and let it simmer for up to 5 minutes. Serve with the chicken and meat remaining.

Jollof Stir-fry Spaghetti

Spaghetti especailly jollof stir-fry spaghetti is one of my best meals ever. I love it because of the mixture of vegetables, chicken, meat and other proteins added to it which brings out the flavour and lovely taste of spaghetti. It can be eaten alone or as an accompaniment to another dish.

Ingredients Amount
Spaghetti(in it's raw state) 1 pack
Tomato paste 1 sachet
Toamto puree As desired
Onions and garlic A bulb each
Salt, pepper and seasonings To taste
Bell pepper 1
Chicken and meat 500g each
Oyster sauce, dark soy sauce 1 tbsp each
Vegetable oil 2 tbsp
Vegetables As desired

Procedure:

  1. Wash, season and boil the meat
  2. Wash, marinate and roast the chicken
  3. Parboil the spaghetti and drain the water from it when it is almost done.
  4. Slice the carrots, spring onions, onions, garlic, ginger and bell peppers and set aside
  5. In a frying pan or wok add little oil followed by onions, garlic and allow to fry till it's looks crunchy
  6. Add the tomato paste, allow to fry a little then then add the toamto puree. Season the mixture, add the oyster sauce and dark soy sauce, salt the mixture then allow to fry.
  7. Slice some of the chicken and meat then add to the mixture and allow to soak in it.
  8. Add the carrots, sweetcorn, bell peppers and green peas into the mixture and stir-fry for a few minutes
  9. Finally add in the spaghetti, stir and allow to simmer a few minutes then serve with the rest fo the chicken and meat.What a lovely meal!!

Meatpie

A meat pie is a pie with a chunky filling of meat and often other savory ingredients. Meat Pie is moist, yummy and has minced meat, potato and carrot filling. The filling is usually adequately seasoned and gives the meat pie its unique taste.

For the meatpie dough:

Ingredients Amount
Flour 700g
Sugar 1 tsp
Salt 1/2 tsp
Butter 380g
Cold water 1/2 cup
Egg 2 large eggs

For the meatpie filling:

Ingredients Amount
Irish potatoes 2 medium sizes
Carrots 1 big sized
Minced meat 500g
Onions, garlic 1 small sized bulb each
Seasonings To taste
Vegetable oil 2 tbsp
Salt To taste
Flour 2 tbsp

Procedure

For the meatpie filling:

  1. Peel the Irish potatoes and scrape the carrots, wash and cut these two into tiny cubes.
  2. Wash and slice the onions into tiny pieces.
  3. With your cooker or stove set to medium heat, heat the vegetable oil in a pot, add the diced onions and stir for a bit, add the minced meat and stir vigorously till the minced meat turns pale.
  4. Add 1 cup of water and seasonings. Cover the pot and once the contents of the pot starts boiling, add the diced carrots and potatoes and cook till everything is well done
  5. Dissolve 2 tablespoons of plain flour in half cup of cold water and add to the meat pie filling. This tip is so that the meat pie filling does not dry up during baking. It is also what keeps the meat pie filling moist
  6. Add salt to taste, stir the contents and turn off the heat. Set the meat pie filling aside and start the preparation of the dough.

For the meatpie dough:

  1. Put the 1 kg of flour in a sizeable bowl; add 2 teaspoons of baking powder and 2 pinches of salt. You really do not need a lot of salt for the meat pie dough. The meat pie will get most of its taste from the filling. Mix these dry ingredients very well.
  2. Use a tablespoon to scoop the margarine in small bits into the bowl of flour. The smaller you make the bits of margarine, the easier it will be for you to rub them into the flour.
  3. Use finger tips to rub the margarine into the flour till the mix becomes like crumbs.
  4. Now, start adding cold water in bits while at the same time folding the mix till a stiff ball of dough is formed. You will be surprised at how small quantity of water you need to get the dough stiff. At most, all you need for the 1 kg of flour is half a cup of water.
  5. Knead the dough very well, put back in the bowl and leave to rest for 5 to 7 minutes. This makes the dough more elastic.

Cut, Fill, Fold and Close

  1. Set your oven to 170°C (335°F) and leave to preheat while you continue with the meat pie.
  2. Rub margarine on the insides of the oven tray and set aside. This is so that the undersides of the meat pies do not burn or stick to the tray during baking.
  3. Break the egg, beat it and set aside, you will need it pretty soon.
  4. Knead the dough some more, roll it out to achieve a 5mm thickness.
  5. Use a cutter, be it the cover of a small pot or a meat pie cutter, to make round cuts on the rolled out dough. Remove the excess dough, leaving behind the round cuts.
  6. Scoop some meat pie filling into the center of the round cuts, the quantity should be such that you can comfortably close the dough without overflows. Don't worry if you over-filled the first one, you have many meat pies to fill today so at some point you will know just the right quantity of meat pie filling to scoop into the dough.
  7. Rub the egg on the inside edge of the cut meatpie dough. Watch the video to see how this is done. This is to ensure that your meat pie is perfectly sealed and does not open up while it is being baked.
  8. Fold one part of the dough to meet the other end and use a fork to press the 2 edges together to close tightly. Place your work of art in the oven tray and repeat the previous step till all the cut out dough is exhausted.
  9. Roll out more dough, cut, fill, close and place in the greased oven tray till all the dough is used.
  10. Rub the egg on the meat pies. This gives the meat pie a golden brown look when done.
  11. Set the tray in the preheated oven and bake for 30 to 40 minutes. This time will depend on the type and heating capacity of your oven. For some ovens, the meat pie will be done in half an hour, for some, it may take up to 50 mins. Whatever type of oven you have, the important thing to know is that the best meat pies are the ones baked at medium heat. You can confirm that the meat pie is done when it starts browning.
